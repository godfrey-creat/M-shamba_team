Challenges in Modern Agriculture

Agribusiness today faces multiple critical problems that reduce both output levels and preservable environmental conditions. Traditional human observations dominate farming activities among most growers, especially in developing areas, thus generating multiple crucial problems.

Soil observation, irrigation monitoring, pest detection, and temperature tracking primarily depend upon human observers during agricultural operations. This monitoring technique absorbs many human workers who can support more beneficial agricultural work.

KALRO reports that poor farm management combined with the failure to provide timely solutions results in excessive crop losses amounting to more than 60%.

Target Users & Beneficiaries

Serving diverse agricultural stakeholders across the spectrum

Small-Scale Farmers

Farmers with 1-10 acres facing challenges including labor shortages, limited access to timely information, and vulnerability to crop losses.

FarmEye offers an affordable entry point to precision agriculture, providing critical data that can significantly improve decision-making.

Large-Scale Commercial Farms

Operations managing extensive land areas where comprehensive monitoring is physically impossible without technological assistance.

FarmEye provides scalable monitoring capabilities that maintain oversight across large areas, standardizing farm management practices.

Agricultural Cooperatives

Organizations representing multiple farmers that face challenges in providing consistent advisory services and ensuring standardized practices.
